\section*{Guidelines}
The document, Annexure - I, was formally adopted on February 20, 2021. It contains the details of all the Councils, Clubs, and Groups (hereinafter referred to as groups$@$IITGN) at the Indian Institute of Technology Gandhinagar. The details include the overview, vision, mission, departments, structure, selection procedure, etc. In addition, this document also contains a list of position of responsibilities (POR's) in the groups$@$IITGN recognized by the Student Senate. Along with it, the procedure to verify the POR's is also mentioned which is applicable to every student and alumni of IIT Gandhinagar. \\

\noindent If a group$@$IITGN wishes to make changes to the existing structure, they are required to bring a proposal to the Student Senate. Upon due deliberation, the Student Senate may decide whether to pass or reject the proposal.\\

\noindent All the groups$@$IITGN are free to make changes to the selection procedure, and departments within the group. It is the responsibility of the group$@$IITGN to update the Convener, Student Senate about the changes/amendments made. In addition, if the changes being made are for longer time period, the Convener, Student Senate will update the document as well.

\subsection{Academic Council}

\subsubsection{Objective}
To ensure proper execution of, and, coordination between various
student-driven academic activities under the aegis of the Academic
Secretary and the supervision of Dean and Associate Deans of Academic
Affairs.

\subsubsection{Structure}
The Student Academic Council is a body of five coordinators and
some additional members. The roles and responsibilities of the
coordinators are described as under:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Coordinator for Academic Discussion Hours (ADH)
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Identifying courses requiring these sessions and competent tutors to conduct them.
        \item Ensuring regular conduct of these sessions, and timely feedback for improvement of the sessions.
        \item Fostering intra and inter-batch discussions on academic topics.
    \end{enumerate}
    \item Coordinator for Peer Assisted Learning (PAL) and Guided Progress Scheme (GPS)
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Establishing communication between PAL tutors and ADH tutors.
        \item Extending required help to students on GPS.
    \end{enumerate}
    \item Coordinator for Events and Initiatives
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Coordinating student-run courses, identifying student requirements and potential tutors for the same.
        \item Coordinating with the Technical and IR\&P Council to arrange for workshops and competitions.
        \item Coordinating events like LaTeX workshop, HackRush, Kya Aap Baarhvi Paas Se Tez Hai? debates, inter- and intra-batch interaction sessions, etc.
    \end{enumerate}
    \item Coordinator for Class Representatives and Course Feedback
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Establishing proper communication with all CRs to sense the need and requirements of students, and implementing solutions to address these needs.
        \item Ensuring appropriate CR performance on the progress of significant activities including course feedback and regular organizing of field visits.
        \item Ensuring that CRs undertake at least one initiative or activity for their discipline’s batch.
        \item Maintaining an archive of minutes of meetings conducted by CRs with their respective batches.
    \end{enumerate}
    \item Coordinator for Documentation and Outreach
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Ensuring documentation of experiences, projects, etc. of past industrial and research internships undertaken by students.
        \item Ensuring documentation of course projects currently being done by students.
        \item Arranging for descriptions of courses, write-ups of course content, and (if applicable) experiences of students who have taken the course in the past.
        \item Maintaining a platform which would feature interesting stuff in the academic spheres of our life. This would aim to fill the vacuum of recognition/celebration of special academic happenings in the entire community.
    \end{enumerate}
    \item Special Advisor
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Provide advice and recommendations to the Academic Secretary on academic matters.
        \item Responsible for acting as a liaison between the Academic Council and the student body, conveying important academic information and seeking feedback.
    \end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}

\noindent The Academic Council also consists of some general members, who assist the coordinators in their work. Additionally, there is a Web-Master who maintains the website of the \href{https://students.iitgn.ac.in/student-academic-council/}{Students Academic Council}. The Web-Master is, by default, a general member of the Council. Academic hobby group representatives are also, by default, members of the Academic Council.

\subsubsection{Selection Eligibility}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item IITGN students from any program and year of study are eligible to apply and fill the nomination form sent to the student community.
    \item Only those who are or have been the class representative can apply for the post of Coordinator for Class Representatives and Course Feedback.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{Selection Procedure} \label{Acad council selection procedure}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Coordinators will be selected through a formal process of nominations and interviews by the Academic Secretary, and the Faculty Advisor\footnote{In case the Faculty Advisor is unavailable, the Academic Secretary will take the interviews.} of the Academic Council.
    \item Selection of general members will also happen through interviews. The panel here would have the Academic Secretary, and the respective coordinator. \label{Acad council selection procedure point 2}
\end{enumerate}

\noindent The above-mentioned selection procedure is for those who apply the nomination form. However, if there is a need for more members or someone wishes to apply later on, one can do so by sending a formal request via email to the Academic Secretary. The selection in this case shall be conducted separately as per \ref{Acad council selection procedure}.\ref{Acad council selection procedure point 2}.

\subsection{Industry Relations \& Projects Council}
\subsubsection{Objective}
The IR\&P Council would lookout for improving industrial-academia relations, spreading awareness about upcoming industrial competitions, promoting entrepreneurship culture, maintaining official web portal and documenting ongoing and previous activities of the council.

\subsubsection{Background}
Students at IITGN drive most of the activities in accordance with the institute’s philosophy of treating its students as responsible adults. Being a research-oriented institute, the students can benefit from industry-driven research projects. Thus, it is postulated to form a student body dedicated to enabling the students to pursue such projects and promote industry-driven inter-disciplinary research in the institute. \\

\noindent Briefly, the role of this council will be to actively contact and attract various industries for projects that the students may undertake. It will keep a record of them and follow up the progress. It will also work towards managing the existing ones in terms of documentation, publicity and initiation. The council will also provide a platform for the development of entrepreneurial activities in the institute.


\subsubsection{Roles and Responsibilities}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Actively contact and collaborate with industries for projects
    \item Encourage students to participate in competitions around the country and the world
    \item Spread awareness about the industry's vision and the challenges they face
    \item Help solve institute level problems by floating relevant projects to the student body
    \item Develop an entrepreneurial ecosystem
    \item Provide platforms for the recognition of students’ work
    \item Documentation of all the previous work done
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{Structure}
The council team will comprise of IR\&P Secretary, 2 advisors, 3 coordinators and 8-10 general members. The roles and responsibilities co-ordinators and general members are as follows:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item IBP and IP Advisors:
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item To help the secretary in conducting the IP’s and IBP’s and to support whenever any guidance or help is required.
        \item To ensure that the previous relations made with the companies and professors are judiciously to ensure better further actions.
    \end{enumerate}
    \item Institute Building Projects (IBP) Coordinator
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Approaching faculties for projects focussed on solving campus-related problems.
        \item Collaborating with nearby colleges to solve their problems.
        \item Ensuring completion of IBP Projects
    \end{enumerate}
    \item Industrial Projects (IP) Coordinator
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Managing all the whereabouts of the industrial projects, which comprises the creation of database, contacting and making proper agreements with the collaborators, collecting feedback from the project guides, and finding suitable mentors for the projects.
        \item Ensuring proper documentation of all the projects.
    \end{enumerate}
    \item Events Coordinator
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Responsible for the planning and marketing of various events such as IndustReal, Hackathon, Webinars/Talks by successful industrialist and entrepreneurs.
        \item Brainstorming and implementing innovative ideas with the help of the team to promote industrial and entrepreneurial culture among the student body.
    \end{enumerate}
    \item General member
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Assisting the secretary and the coordinators for conducting all the activities of the council.
        \item Actively contact and collaborate with industries for projects.
        \item Encourage students to participate in competitions around the country and the world.
        \item Spread awareness about the industry's vision and the challenges they face.
        \item Provide platforms for the recognition of students’ work.
        \item Documentation of all the previous work done.
    \end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}


\subsubsection{Selection Eligibility}
IITGN students from any program and year of study are eligible to apply and fill the nomination form sent to the student community. \\

\noindent Students applying for the post of Special Advisors should have at least one year of experience working with the IR\&P council, either as a team member, or a coordinator.

\subsubsection{Selection Procedure}
The members will be selected through a formal process of nominations
and group discussions/interviews by the present IR\&P secretary and the previous IR\&P secretary.

\subsection{Professional Development Council}
\subsubsection{Objective}
To provide the student body with a structured framework for their overall professional development, to spread about it and to create avenues for the same. PDC works on the following principles:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item To assess the current performance of IITGN students in professional aspects and create a platform in order to improve upon it.
    \item To promote a healthy culture among the students of IITGN with regard to professional skill development.
    \item To act as a medium of communication between the Career Development Services and the student body. This would help in better communication of the feedback from the companies to the students and to also raise, address the concerns of the students to the CDS.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{Background}
All the student activities at IITGN are led and driven by students in
accordance with the Institute's philosophy of treating its students as responsible adults. Students have shown commendable leadership and
decision-making skills during the previous years through various activities and initiatives (Amalthea, Blithchron, NYASA, PAL, etc. to name a few). However, there is a need to expose the students to professionalism and help them develop skills that could cater to their career goals. Thus, it is postulated to form a student body dedicated to looking after the various professional development aspects of student life thereby leading to the overall enrichment of an individual's student life at IITGN.


\subsubsection{Potential Activities}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Creating awareness among the student body regarding their career paths and choices.
    \item Organizing relevant professional development activities (e.g. mock tests, interviews, soft skills workshops, etc) based on the needs of the students.
    \item Motivating individuals to develop professional skills and guiding them accordingly with the help of experts.
    \item Keeping the record of activities conducted and conducting follow-up activities based on the impact.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{Structure}
The PDC will be a body of three coordinators, a special advisor and some additional members. The roles and responsibilities of the members are described as under: 
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Coordinator for Industrial Training and Preparation
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Preparing, documenting and compiling the Preparation Material for the Placements and Industrial Internships.
        \item Coordinating with the CDS to organize preparatory sessions, mock tests, mock interviews, etc. for Placements and Internships.
    \end{enumerate}
    \item Coordinator for Career Exposure and Guidance
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Planning and organizing different events and workshops for the overall exposure and professional development of the students.
        \item Coordinating with the Career Guides and ensuring that students get regular career counselling about their career and guidance about various opportunities.
        \item Coordinating with the CDS and the faculty to organize industrial visits for all departments.
    \end{enumerate}
    \item Coordinator for Research and Higher Education
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Preparing, documenting and compiling the Preparation Material for Academic Internships and Higher Education in Collaboration with the Student Academic Council.
        \item Organizing research based events and  preparatory workshops for Post-Graduate students.
        \item Managing the Focused group Initiatives for preparation of competitive examinations like CAT, UPSC, GATE etc.
    \end{enumerate}
    \item Special Advisor:
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item The Special Advisor will offer advice and recommendations to the PDC Secretary concerning student career matters.
        \item Acting as a bridge between the PDC and the student body, the Special Advisor will communicate important information and gather feedback to ensure effective collaboration.
    \end{enumerate}
    \item General member
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Assisting the Secretary and Coordinators in carrying out all the activities of the council.
        \item Organise relevant professional development activities and skill development workshops based on needs of students.
        \item Actively contact seniors and alumni for documenting the preparatory data and conducting events.
        \item Spreading awareness of the council activities, events, material and career opportunities among the students.
        \item \textbf{Webmaster}: Responsible for keeping the PDC  website up-to-date and running. 
        \item \textbf{Social Media Manager:} Maintaining the Instagram/Facebook/LinkedIn Social Media Handles, creating content to post for eg. podcasts, talks, etc.
    \end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{Selection Eligibility}
IITGN students from any program and year of study are eligible to apply and fill the nomination form sent to the student community. Only those with a minimum of one year of experience as an active member of the PDC/Annuity/TEDx can apply for the post of Special Advisor.

\subsubsection{Selection Procedure}
The members will be selected through a formal process of nominations
and group discussions/ interviews by the present PDC secretary and the previous PDC secretary.

\subsection{Technical Council}

\subsubsection{Objective}
Technical Council strives hard to provide the students with a platform to learn and critically understand the technologies that aid innovation and development, along with providing the resources that facilitates it. The Council wishes to create an environment that creates future leaders in technology that make an impact on the world around us and also developing the culture that can facilitate doing so.

\subsubsection{Potential Activities}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Students Summer Technical Projects (SSTP) (for at least 50 days): BTech First Years work on developing their prototypes during the summer vacations
    \item Winter Projects (20 days): Utilization of December vacations to gain acquaintance with different skills that will help in later semesters in large scale projects
    \item Tech Radio: Series of small talks on projects happening in the college
    \item IGNITE: The annual technical fair which brings out the best of technical skills gained by the students around the year
    \item Technical Week: An opportunity to come closer to the various domains
    \item Participation in External Competitions
    \item Pan IIT Initiatives: Tech Board Meetings, Inter IIT Tech Meet
    \item Development of institute apps and websites
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{Structure}
The Technical Council consists of the Technical Secretary, Coordinators, Club Secretaries, App and Website Manager, General Members, as well as the coordinators for Tinkerers' Lab. The roles and responsibilities of the various PORs are described as under:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Technical Coordinator
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Ensure the seamless execution and logistics of technical elements and resources in events, projects, and activities.
        \item Work closely with various clubs and councils to develop event ideas and brainstorm innovative concepts.
        \item Collaborate with the Management Coordinator on financial matters, including securing approvals and managing budgets.
        \item Prepare reports/documentation on technical aspects of events and projects, collect feedback and assess new technologies for potential benefit to the council
    \end{enumerate}
    \item Management Coordinator
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Assist the Technical Council in managing and coordinating events, projects, and other collaborative activities.
        \item Oversee event logistics, including vendor management, service provider coordination, and handling the finances, to ensure seamless execution.
        \item Manage the council's resources, including inventory management, procurement, and maintenance of equipment and materials.
        \item Develop and maintain schedules, timelines, and deadlines for events and projects together with the Technical Coordinator.
    \end{enumerate}
    \item Outreach Coordinator
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Develop and implement outreach strategies to promote the Technical Council's events, projects, and activities, increasing visibility and engagement.
        \item Build and maintain relationships with industry partners and sponsors to secure resources and support for council initiatives.
        \item Establish and maintain connections with other educational institutions, local communities, and other external stakeholders to foster collaborative projects and knowledge-sharing.
    \end{enumerate}
    \item Club Secretaries and Other Group Coordinators:  There are 6 technical clubs and every club has a secretary who is responsible for the functioning of the club.  Various technical clubs are as follows:
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Metis - Coding Club
        \item Digis - Digital Sports Club
        \item Mean Mechanics - Robotics Club
        \item Odyssey - Astronomy Club
        \item GRASP - Competitive Programming Group
        \item Anveshanam - Research Society
    \end{enumerate}
    Refer section \ref{Technical Clubs} for the details of every club. Apart from these clubs, there are two other groups associated with the Technical Council, i.e., Tinkerers' Lab, and Torque, which is the annual institute student magazine. Refer to sections \ref{Tinkerers' Lab} and \ref{Torque} for further details.
    \item App and Website Manager: Responsible for managing and maintaining the council website and other apps and websites that are rolled out by the Technical Council for the community.
    \item General Members
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Develop and take charge of innovative events and projects that align with the council's objectives, driving growth and impact.
        \item Assist in brainstorming event-specific details and execution, translating conceptualised ideas into tangible experiences (especially for the clubs).
        \item Contribute to the council's decision-making process and help shape its direction.
        \item Provide support to coordinators and ensure smooth event operations, acting as a point of contact during events.
    \end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}

Apart from this, the Tinkerers’ Lab, Drone Society, and hobby groups that have been working on various genres of projects and activities fall under the Technical Council.

\subsubsection{Selection Eligibility}
IITGN students from any program and year of study are eligible to apply and fill the nomination form sent to the student community.

\subsubsection{Selection Procedure}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item All positions in the council may be selected by various processes such as SOPs, Interviews, Resumes, and so on, by the Technical Secretary, advisably in consultation with the previous members of the council.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Welfare Council}
\subsubsection{Objective}
Protect and ensure student welfare.

\subsubsection{Roles and responsibilities}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Proper operations of mess and other food outlets in the campus.
    \item Proper operations of laundry services.
    \item Proper addressal of issues related to (but not limited to) hostel infrastructure and facilities.
    \item Regular collection of feedback about all the above facilities.
    \item Maintaining proper health facilities in the hostel and academic area.
    \item Developing and maintaining proper services/infrastructure/portals based on student needs.
    \item Proper documentation of all activities, discussions, mistakes, recommendations and ideas taken by the Welfare Council. General guidelines for the list of things that can be covered in the documentation are:
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Hostel, Canteens and Mess Complaints
        \item Annual Budget (to be prepared at the start of the tenure)
        \item Initiatives (with details including number of participants), important dates, people involved in the initiative, etc
        \item Any other information/discussion that is relevant to the functioning of the future councils.
    \end{enumerate}
    % \item The Welfare Secretary will be a permanent member of committees such as HFMC, CEMC, Transportation Committee and AMC (or any equivalent committee in the future). The committee based on recommendations from the Welfare Secretary may invite respective incharges or general members.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{Structure}
The Student Welfare Council shall consist of the Welfare Secretary, Special Advisor, Mess Secretary, 4 Coordinators, general members and a few senators. The roles and responsibilities of the coordinators are described as under:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Advisor to the Welfare Secretary
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Assist the Secretary on all issues and terms.
        \item Reduce the load on the Welfare Secretary.
    \end{enumerate}
    \item Mess Secretary, Incharge of Mess Operations
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Responsible for proper operation of mess facilities
        \item Collect regular feedback (through existing Instant Feedback System or otherwise)
        \item Ensure highest quality of hygiene is maintained in mess
        \item Mess allocation is done at a fixed frequency
        \item The mess menu is regularly updated with sufficient variety
    \end{enumerate}
    \item Mess Coordinator
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Operating under the Mess Secretary, specifically manage individual mess operations.
        \item Gathering detailed feedback from students, ensuring hygiene standards maintenance, and promptly addressing any mess-related issues.
    \end{enumerate}
    \item Outlet Coordinator, Incharge of Commercial Outlets
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Responsible for proper operation of all commercial outlets (including but not limited to canteens, grocery stores, temporary establishments such as food trucks in Student Hostels, Academic Block and Central Arcade)
        \item Collect regular feedback (through existing QR System or otherwise)
        \item Ensure highest quality of hygiene and quality of products is maintained in all the outlets
        \item Survey prices and quality of food served and other items sold around the campus as well as in other colleges (including but not limited to IITs).
        \item Search for new potential vendors in case existing vendors are discontinuing operations.
        \item Ensure sufficient variety in food and other items provided in canteens
    \end{enumerate}
    \item Laundry Coordinator, Incharge of Laundry Operations
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Ensure proper operation of Laundry Services in campus
        \item Address all issues and complaints related to Laundry in campus
        \item Ensure that the services provided are of the highest quality
        \item Organize regular checks to the Laundry Site for inspection
    \end{enumerate}
    \item Hostel Coordinator, Incharge of Hostel Maintenance
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Address any issues (such as washrooms, room furniture etc) that students have
        \item Document all issues raised, actions taken on the complaint and required follow-ups taken up by the incharge, staff or complainer.
    \end{enumerate}
    \item Student Health Coordinator, Incharge of Student Health

\subsection{Green Club}
\subsubsection{Background and Objectives}
IIT Gandhinagar is one of the most efficient campuses in keeping the benchmark in green
development through different initiatives. The Institute has integrated various green initiatives
to maximize the use of available space and preserve the campus's biodiversity and aesthetic
value. The Green Office initiatives help the student community work towards or even develop
innovative startup ideas; however, without community support, the activities of Green Office
have a limited impact. Through the creation of a Green Club, we propose to actively engage the
students of IIT Gandhinagar to help minimize the impact of the campus on the environment.
Students can use this opportunity to share their love for nature, innovate solutions to reduce
waste, and decrease the overall carbon footprint of the Institute. The primary objectives of such
a club are to:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Build awareness in the student and Housing community about climate change and
    sustainability action through an intersection of society, market, and environmental
    science.
    \item Engage students at an early age in climate action, leading to informed daily choices that
    continue long after leaving the campus. Such a change is beneficial for the country as a
    whole.
    \item Innovate creative solutions to reduce the impact of the student lifestyle (different
    options).
    \item Encourage assisting local communities in selling sustainable products and services.
\end{enumerate}

    \item General Member Team : The general members will carry out different on-site activities. The team will coordinate with the entire club in organizing and executing the required activities proposed by the Convenor. Halla Bol is the annual intra-college sports festival of IIT Gandhinagar. Ever since its inception in 2010, each Halla Bol edition has recorded the highest number of student, faculty and staff participation, as compared to other IIT Gandhinagar intra-college sports festivals.

The IIT Gandhinagar student body, represented by the Halla Bol Organizing Committee, wishes to achieve the following set of goals, through Halla Bol ‘18:
